THE RESEARCH PROCESS

HOW IT CAME TO BE

I drew inspiration from the visual culture of the Philippines; the expressive calligraphy and handpainted letters commonly seen on jeepneys, which are iconic symbols of Filipino street life.

I began by experimenting with plaka paint and black ink using different brushes to replicate the organic strokes found in Filipino sign painting. This process allowed me to explore the fluidity and rhythm of hand-drawn calligraphy, mimicking the way paint naturally pools and tapers with pressure.

Once I was satisfied with the inked letters, I scanned and digitized them, carefully refining the edges while maintaining the imperfect charm of the original strokes.
